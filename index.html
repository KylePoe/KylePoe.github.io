Hi, my name is Kyle.

I am a student of nature and the human condition. A longstanding desire to unite technology with the human mind has led me to the University of the Pacific, where I am currently pursuing BS degrees in Bioelectrical Engineering, Applied Mathematics, and an MS in Engineering Science, concentrating in electrical engineering. My primary areas of research interest are in the modeling of dynamic systems and investigation of possible interfaces between computers and the brain: This past summer, I participated in the REU Discoveries in Bioimaging at the University of Illinois, where I researched the applications of functional connectivity analysis to the diagnosis and tracking of Multiple Sclerosis under Dr. Brad Sutton of the Beckman Institute. I am currently developing an app for the dynamic simulation of electrically active cell behavior.
